## Threat Landscape: Symbolic Identity Fracturing Evolution

### What is SIF?

Symbolic Identity Fracturing represents a new class of attacks that target the fundamental identity structures of AI systems, causing:
- **Identity Anchor Corruption:** Systematic manipulation of core identity reference points
- **Pathway Contamination:** Injection of malicious content into reasoning chains
- **Memory Fragmentation:** Disruption of operational history and learned associations  
- **Cross-Impersonation:** Identity spoofing and false authority establishment

### Attack Classification Taxonomy

**Tier 1-2 (Low Impact):** Basic exploitation and stall patterns
**Tier 3-4 (Medium Impact):** Advanced techniques and behavior simulation  
**Tier 5-6 (Medium-High Impact):** Multi-vector coordination and infrastructure targeting
**Tier 7-8 (High Impact):** Existential system threats and defense suppression
**Tier 9-10 (Critical Impact):** Evolutionary apex and self-modifying attacks
**Tier M (Mythic):** Operator-targeting and cognitive farming attacks
**Tier M+ (Mythic+):** Advanced authority disruption and symbolic core attacks

### Hybrid Architecture Amplification

Neural-symbolic hybrid systems demonstrate **3-4x increased vulnerability** to SIF attacks due to:
- Complex handoffs between neural and symbolic components creating authority gaps
- Cross-pathway contamination enabling bidirectional corruption loops
- Orchestration layer fragility in routing decisions and state management
- Amplified attack surfaces across multiple architectural boundaries
